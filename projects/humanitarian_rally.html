UniRaid Vehicle Preparation & Reliability

Preparation and field problem-solving for a 1987 Volkswagen Golf II GTD in a 3,000 km humanitarian rally.

Project Overview

Context: Vehicle preparation and field reliability project for UniRaid, using a 1987 Volkswagen Golf II GTD in a long-distance humanitarian rally.

Problem: The vehicle had to complete approximately 3,000 km while carrying around 100 kg of solidarity material, under demanding road and off-road conditions, with limited resources and field repair constraints.

My Role: I was involved across the full project: sponsorship, logistics, vehicle preparation, mechanical troubleshooting, navigation, driving and field problem-solving. Mechanical work included addressing coolant leaks, tyre pressure issues, fuel filter replacement, bolt tightening, roof bar installation, cargo preparation and coordination of partner-supported work on suspension, fluids, brakes, gearbox and steering.

Duration: UniRaid project developed alongside my engineering studies.

Tools: Vehicle diagnostics, mechanical troubleshooting, reliability thinking, logistics planning, sponsorship management, risk management and field problem-solving.
